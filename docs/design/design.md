# BookOS Design System & Product UX Brief

Version 1.0

## Product Overview

BookOS is a Service Commerce OS for SMBs.

The platform helps service businesses convert social traffic into confirmed bookings through:

* Smart Pages
* Service Catalogs
* Scheduling
* Deposits
* WhatsApp Automation
* CRM
* Analytics

The product is mobile-first and optimized for business owners who primarily operate from smartphones.

Primary markets:

* Beauty salons
* Aesthetic clinics
* Nail studios
* Lash & brow studios

Future expansion:

* Dental
* Fitness
* Wellness
* Education
* Photography

---

# Brand Personality

BookOS should feel:

* Professional
* Reliable
* Fast
* Modern
* Premium
* Operational

Avoid:

* Cute
* Playful
* Consumer-social
* Enterprise-corporate
* Overly feminine beauty branding

Think:

Stripe + Linear + Shopify

Not:

Canva + Instagram + TikTok

---

# Core Product Principles

## Principle 1

Conversion First

Every screen should help users reach a confirmed booking.

---

## Principle 2

Speed Over Decoration

Users should always know:

* Where they are
* What comes next
* How to finish the task

---

## Principle 3

Mobile First

Design mobile screens first.

Desktop is an enhancement.

Target widths:

* Mobile: 390px
* Tablet: 768px
* Desktop: 1440px

---

## Principle 4

Premium Simplicity

Whitespace should create quality.

Do not rely on gradients, glassmorphism, or decorative effects.

# Customer Experience

## Objective

Convert a visitor into a confirmed booking within 2 minutes.

---

## Booking Flow

Landing Page
→ Service Catalog
→ Service Detail
→ Select Date
→ Select Time
→ Select Staff
→ Booking Form
→ Review
→ Payment
→ Success

---

## Customer Design Guidelines

Large tap targets.

Sticky CTA buttons.

Minimal form fields.

Visible progress indicator.

Trust signals:

* Secure payment
* Verified business
* WhatsApp confirmation

---

# Dashboard Home

Components:

* Today's bookings
* Pending confirmations
* Upcoming appointments
* Revenue summary
* Quick actions

---

# Calendar

Most important admin screen.

Requirements:

* Day view
* Week view
* Staff filter
* Color-coded booking statuses
* Fast reschedule interactions

Calendar should feel closer to Google Calendar than a traditional admin table.

---

# Booking Status Colors

Pending:
Amber

Confirmed:
Blue

Completed:
Green

Cancelled:
Red

No Show:
Gray

---

# Service Cards

Structure:

Image
Service Name
Duration
Price
Book CTA

Cards should prioritize booking conversion over content density.

---

# Form Design

Single-column layout.

One primary action per screen.

Inline validation.

Large input fields.

Phone number optimized for WhatsApp entry.

---

# Empty States

Every empty state should educate users.

Example:

"No bookings today"

Show:

Create Booking CTA

instead of blank space.

---

# Analytics

Simple.

Avoid enterprise BI aesthetics.

Focus on:

* Bookings
* Conversion
* Revenue
* Repeat Customers
* No Shows

---

# Motion

Use subtle motion only.

Examples:

* Button hover
* Page transitions
* Success animations

Avoid:

* Parallax
* Fancy loading effects
* Excessive animation

---

# Overall Design Goal

If Stripe built a booking platform for service businesses in Southeast Asia, this is what BookOS should feel like.

The experience should communicate:

"Professional enough for a clinic.
Simple enough for a salon owner.
Fast enough for Instagram traffic."
